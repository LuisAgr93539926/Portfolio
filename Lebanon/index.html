Lebanon: The Pearl of the Middle East

Bonjour, c'est le Liban. Le Liban a beaucoup d'histoire et de culture. Des gens l'appellent la 'Suisse de l'Est'. Le Liban est près de la mer Méditerranée. Il y a beaucoup de choses anciennes et nouvelles. Le Liban est grand. Il y a environ 10 452 kilomètres carrés. Il y a environ 6,8 millions de personnes. Il y a beaucoup de cultures et de religions différentes. Les gens parlent arabe. Ils parlent aussi anglais et français. Le drapeau a trois bandes. Il y a du rouge en haut et en bas. Il y a du blanc au milieu. Il y a un arbre vert (un cèdre) au milieu. Les gens disent : "Tout pour le pays, la gloire et le drapeau."

Fun facts:

  • Lebanon is famous for its Cedar trees, which are a central figure on the country's flag. These trees have been valued for their timber since ancient times, notably in the construction of Phoenician sea-faring vessels.
  • Lebanon is the oldest country name in the world in continuous use for over 4000 years.
  • The country has 18 recognized religious sects. It's one of the most religiously diverse countries in the Middle East.

The Unique Charm of Lebanon

Lebanon, known as the 'Pearl of the Middle East', is a country of captivating contrasts. Here, the echoes of ancient civilizations live harmoniously with the vibrancy of modern life. Lebanon's uniqueness stems from its rich historical tapestry, diverse culture, and stunning geography. Nestled on the eastern shore of the Mediterranean Sea, it's a place where snow-capped mountains meet golden beaches, offering a myriad of activities for nature and adventure enthusiasts. Visitors can explore the ancient Roman ruins at Baalbek, wander through the Phoenician city of Byblos , or soak in the cosmopolitan charm of Beirut, the capital city often dubbed as the 'Paris of the Middle East'.

"Lebanon is more than a country, it is a message of freedom and an example of pluralism for East and West."
Pope John Paul II during his visit to Lebanon in 1997. Reference

The Taste of Tradition: Lebanese Cuisine

Lebanese cuisine, a gastronomic celebration of rich flavors and fresh ingredients, serves as a mirror to the country's heritage. The most popular traditional dishes are a testament to Lebanon's culinary prowess. Hummus , a creamy spread made from mashed chickpeas, and Tabbouleh, a refreshing salad made with bulgur, tomatoes, finely chopped parsley, mint, and onions, are staples in any Lebanese meal. Another crowd favorite is Shawarma, a marinated meat dish that can be served on a platter or in a sandwich. These dishes, coupled with the warm Lebanese hospitality, make every meal a memorable experience.

"My Lebanon is a serene mountain sitting between the sea and the plains, like a poet between one eternity and another."
Kahlil Gibran, Lebanese-American writer, poet, and artist. Reference
